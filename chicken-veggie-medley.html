üë®‚Äçüç≥ Step by Step Method

  1. Right, let's get started! First, wash your hands thoroughly and give all your vegetables a good rinse. Cut the chicken into small, toddler-appropriate pieces - think smaller than a grape!
  2. Heat that lovely olive oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat. We want it nice and warm but not smoking - perfect for gentle cooking!
  3. Add your chicken pieces to the pan and cook for 5-6 minutes, turning occasionally until they're beautifully golden and cooked right through. No pink bits allowed for our little ones!
  4. Now for the vegetables! Add your diced carrots first as they take a bit longer. Cook for 3-4 minutes until they start to soften nicely.
  5. Pop in those lovely broccoli florets and add a splash of water. Cover the pan and let everything steam together for 4-5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Add the butter and give everything a gentle stir. The butter helps make everything silky and delicious - just what little taste buds love!
  7. Let everything cool to the perfect temperature for tiny mouths. Test it yourself first - we want happy faces, not surprised ones!

Frequently Asked Questions - Chicken & Veggie Medley

How do I know if the chicken is cooked properly for my toddler?

The chicken should reach an internal temperature of 75¬∞C and have no pink areas. Cut into the thickest piece to check - the juices should run clear, not pink. When in doubt, cook a little longer!

What vegetables work best for this recipe?

Soft vegetables that cook quickly work best - try sweet potato, cauliflower, peas, or courgette. Avoid hard vegetables like raw carrots unless they're cooked until very soft.

Can I use frozen vegetables instead of fresh?

Absolutely! Frozen vegetables are often more convenient and just as nutritious. Add them directly to the pan - no need to defrost first. They might need an extra minute or two of cooking time.

My toddler won't eat the vegetables - what can I do?

Try serving the vegetables separately at first, or mix tiny amounts into foods they already enjoy. Keep offering them - it can take 10+ exposures before a child will try something new!

How should I cut the food for different ages?

For 6-9 months: soft strips they can grasp. For 9-12 months: small soft pieces they can pincer grasp. For 12+ months: gradually increase size as their chewing skills develop.
