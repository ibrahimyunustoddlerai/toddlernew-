üë®‚Äçüç≥ Step by Step Method

  1. Right then, let's start by giving that lovely sweet potato a good wash and peel. Cut it into small cubes - about the size of your thumbnail is perfect for even cooking!
  2. Pop the rice in a small saucepan with plenty of water and bring it to a gentle boil. We want it lovely and soft for little mouths - about 15-20 minutes should do it!
  3. While the rice is cooking, steam your sweet potato cubes for 12-15 minutes until they're fork-tender. You should be able to mash them easily with no effort at all.
  4. Once everything's beautifully soft, drain the rice well and let it cool slightly. We don't want any burns on little tongues!
  5. Mash the sweet potato with that lovely butter until it's creamy and smooth. Add a tiny pinch of cinnamon if you fancy - it brings out the natural sweetness!
  6. Gently fold the cooked rice into the mashed sweet potato. Add a splash of milk if you want it extra creamy - just enough to get the perfect consistency.
  7. Let everything cool to the perfect temperature for your little one. Give it a gentle stir and taste-test yourself - it should be warm, not hot!

üí° Picky Eater Alternatives

Frequently Asked Questions - Sweet Potato & Rice Bowl

How do I know when the sweet potato is soft enough?

Test with a fork - it should pierce easily with no resistance, and you should be able to mash it effortlessly. If there's any firmness, cook for a few more minutes.

Can I use white rice instead of brown rice?

Absolutely! White rice works perfectly and often cooks faster. However, brown rice provides more fiber and nutrients, so it's worth trying if your toddler will eat it.

How long can I store leftovers?

Store in the refrigerator for up to 3 days in an airtight container. Reheat gently in the microwave, adding a splash of milk if it seems dry. Freeze portions for up to 3 months.

My toddler finds this too bland - can I add seasoning?

Try a tiny pinch of mild spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, or even a small amount of mild curry powder. Avoid salt and strong spices for very young toddlers.

What's the best consistency for different ages?

For 6-9 months: completely smooth puree. For 9-12 months: slightly lumpy but still very soft. For 12+ months: can be chunkier with soft rice grains visible.
