üë®‚Äçüç≥ Step by Step Method

  1. Right then, let's start with that lovely ripe banana! Mash it up in a bowl until it's lovely and smooth - this is perfect for little helpers to get involved with!
  2. Add your oats to the mashed banana and give it a good stir. The oats will start to soften beautifully as they absorb all those natural banana juices.
  3. Pour in the milk gradually, stirring as you go. You want a creamy, porridge-like consistency that's perfect for tiny mouths.
  4. If using egg (for toddlers 12m+), crack it in and whisk everything together until well combined. This adds lovely protein and makes it extra creamy!
  5. Heat your coconut oil in a non-stick pan over low-medium heat. We're cooking gently here - no rushing!
  6. Spoon the mixture into the pan and cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring gently to create soft, scrambled textures.
  7. Once it's warmed through and slightly set, remove from heat and let it cool to the perfect temperature for your little one.

Frequently Asked Questions - Banana Oat Scramble

Can I make this without eggs for younger babies?

Absolutely! For babies under 12 months, simply omit the egg. The banana and oats create a lovely creamy texture on their own, and it's still packed with nutrition.

What if my banana isn't ripe enough?

If your banana is a bit firm, try mashing it with a fork and adding a tiny splash of warm milk to help soften it. Alternatively, microwave the banana for 10-15 seconds to soften it naturally.

How can I make this recipe more interesting for my toddler?

Try adding a pinch of cinnamon, mashing in some berries, or serving with small pieces of soft fruit on the side. You can also let them help with the mashing - involvement often leads to eating!

Can I prepare this the night before?

Yes! Prepare the mixture (without cooking) and store in the fridge overnight. The oats will soften beautifully. Just warm gently in the morning or serve cold as overnight oats.

Is this suitable for baby-led weaning?

Perfect for BLW! The soft, mashable texture is ideal for self-feeding. You can also form it into small patties and lightly cook them for easier finger-food handling.
